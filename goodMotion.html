Premise

What is sedentary lifestyle ?

A sedentary lifestyle is one that involves very little or no physical exercise, wherein most of the day is spent sitting, lying down and expending very little energy.

A sedentary lifestyle is characterized by spending six or more hours per day sitting or lying down, with very little to no exercise. (from Google Search)

This case study is about coming up with a solution to tackle sedentary lifestyle in order to improve health & well being.

A Sleepy Cat’s picture denoting sedentary lifestyle.

Why is it such big thing ?

A sedentary lifestyle is a major concern for public health because it is linked to many chronic health problems, such as obesity, type 2 diabetes, some types of cancer, heart disease, and early death. When you spend a lot of time sitting or lying down without much physical activity, your body has trouble controlling blood pressure, regulating blood sugar, and breaking down fat. This can lead to a range of health issues, including:

  • Increased risk of all-cause mortality
  • Doubled risk of cardiovascular diseases, diabetes, and obesity
  • Increased risk of colon cancer, high blood pressure, osteoporosis, lipid disorders, depression, and anxiety

Approximately 2 million deaths per year are attributed to physical inactivity, and 60 to 85% of people worldwide lead sedentary lifestyles. In simple terms, a sedentary lifestyle is bad for your health and can lead to serious health problems.

How can you tackle a sedentary lifestyle ?

The solutions is pretty intuitive, Do More Physical Activity.

To tackle a sedentary lifestyle, you can try the following strategies:

  • Incorporate more physical activity: Engage in activities like swimming, high-intensity interval training, and cycling to break free from a sedentary lifestyle.
  • Stand when you can: Many activities can be done standing up, such as chores around the house, gardening, or taking phone calls while walking
  • Take breaks to move: Get up and walk around during television commercials, or take the stairs instead of using the elevator.
  • Find active hobbies: Participate in activities you enjoy, such as dance classes, indoor rock climbing, or other sports.
  • Add small bits of exercise to your daily routine: Work small bits of exercise into your day, such as taking a 30-minute walk three days a week, broken down into smaller segments if needed.
  • Stay hydrated: Drinking enough water throughout the day can help you move more and stay active.

Remember, even small changes can make a big difference in reducing the negative effects of a sedentary lifestyle. It's essential to find activities you enjoy and make them a part of your daily routine.
